Staff recruitment

An appropriate, comprehensive and rigorous staff recruitment and selection process is essential to protect individuals, staff, the organisation and the applicant from the effects of a making a poor appointment. You must make every effort to implement recruitment policies and processes that:

  1. Are up to date.
  2. Are planned to achieve positive outcomes for individuals.
  3. Emphasise a commitment to safeguarding individuals.
  4. Focus on appointing staff with appropriate values, knowledge, skills and qualifications.
  5. Include a face to face interview for all shortlisted candidates based on an objective assessment of the candidates ability to meet the person specification and job description.
  6. Meet legal and regulatory requirements.
  7. Are fair and non- discriminatory.

You must check criminal records and relevant registers, any gaps in employment history and ensure that the person is legally entitled to work in the UK. You must seek reliable references. You should ensure that staff are provided with an up to date written job description and person specification that outlines their role, responsibilities and accountabilities.

If you are in any doubt about safe recruitment and selection and reaching a confident selection of a suitable candidate, you must seek appropriate advice and expertise, e.g., from Human Resources or legal services.

You should involve individuals who use services in the recruitment and selection process, where possible.
