Supervision and appraisal of staff

All your staff, whether they are full or part time, need supervision and appraisal to guide, support, motivate and enable them to develop good practice. You must implement supervision and appraisal processes that meet legal and regulatory requirements. You should encourage staff to fully participate in supervision and appraisal processes. You should ensure that supervision and appraisal processes:

  1. promote best practice with individuals
  2. ensure staff are clear about their roles, responsibilities and accountabilities
  3. ensure staff are clear about the standards of conduct and performance expected of them
  4. ensure staff are clear about agreed actions
  5. provide constructive feedback to staff on their practice
  6. encourage staff to develop reflective practice including reflecting on and learning from mistakes
  7. address conduct and performance that falls below required standards
  8. assist staff to identify areas for learning and development
  9. support staff to manage their workloads

You should be honest and objective when supervising and appraising staff.

You should ensure that supervision and appraisal records meet regulatory and organisational requirements and are accurate, complete, accessible, signed and dated.

You should ensure staff have access to specialist supervision, support and advice, where necessary.
